About me

My interests growing up was playing video games, watching anime, reading manga and webtoons etc -- which eventually grew into attempting to draw and sketch such media. I enjoyed discovering new art mediums but I always stuck to the same cheap and affordable things: pens.

I drew almost everyday, tracing, copying, creating original character designs inspired from various pop culture.

My biggest inspiration when I was growing up were the works of Akira Amano (Katekyo Hitman Reborn! and Psycho-Pass character design).

Travels

I planned my first ever 3 week trip to Japan. I headed to Tokyo first for the first 6 days which was where I jumpstarted my stationery shopping. The first pen I got was the Pilot Kakuno in the family series. I had to buy a converter and a Pilot Blue Black ink to go with it as well.

Here are some of the stationery stores that I visited:

  • Kurashiki TSL for custom Traveler's Co; located in Kurashiki
  • Loft -- very expansive department like store. Largest store in Shibuya, Tokyo.
  • Tobichi in Kyoto for Hobonichi planners and accessories
  • Ancora in Ginza Tokyo for custom Sailor pens and Ink station
  • Itoya for premium stationery, total of 18 floors in Ginza

Journaling

One of the things I do to make use of all the pens I have is to journal for wellness and day to day reflection. I use stickers for mood tracking or general goal setting, and I write with fountain pens of various ink colours, ballpoints for fast drying or gel pens for colourful inks in a fast drying formula.

This year (2025), I have been using my Hobonichi English edition in A6. I have the pencil board, a custom cover and cover on cover to protect the fabric. The only issue I have is that I bought a smaller size since I really don't have that much to do during the day... but I like to sketch so that is also a great way of filling up the page. I already splurged on two planners next year in a weekly slim size notebook and a traditional A5 size as well.
